WPL1 Situation & Reflection

DVO Profile & Learning Objectives

My development within the digital designer roles during Workplace Learning 1.

The Graphic Designer

I have proven that I can translate client wishes into a visual identity. Projects like 'Klein Genot', 'Autogarage', and 'BarberMo' have taught me to work from concept to a full design.

The Digital Expert

In 'BrightNews', I am working on a large-scale project featuring backend logic and AI integration for content filtering. I also learned to technically build this portfolio site from start to finish.

The Creative Communicator

I learned to proactively request feedback from instructors. My next step is to present projects live to others to communicate my vision even more effectively.

The Enterprising Professional

I gained deep insight into project management: from estimating time to monitoring progress. I now better understand the effort required for a quality final product.

AIM-Reflection

Introduction

The past semester of Workplace Learning 1 focused on creating a professional site and working with real-world projects, which I found highly engaging.

Agile (Positive)

I loved designing and organizing projects from start to finish, aiming for maximum efficiency. This process felt natural, though I learned a great deal from it.

Less Positive (Challenges)

While mostly positive, I sometimes faced code that didn't work perfectly. Diving deeper into the code to solve unknown issues was challenging but very educational.

Insights

I gained insight into the time and effort required for web projects. I now see how I can work faster and more efficiently in the future by learning from what went less well.

Mentor (Pride)

I am very proud of my website and will show it with pride. It serves as an ideal business card that leaves a lasting impression.

What do I take to WPL2?

Learning Goals

I aim to ask for intermediate feedback more often. I tend to wait until something is finished, which leaves little time for major improvements.

Soft Skills

There is room for improvement in Figma, such as using more components to create a more structured and professional design workflow.

Core Quadrants

1. Growth & Feedback

  • Quality: Drive
  • Pitfall: Asking for help too late
  • Challenge: Regular check-ins
  • Allergy: Passivity

2. Technical Design

  • Quality: Eye for detail
  • Pitfall: Lack of structure
  • Challenge: Component-based design
  • Allergy: Sloppiness

3. Creativity

  • Quality: Innovative drive
  • Pitfall: Over-engineering (too many extras)
  • Challenge: Focus on essence
  • Allergy: Standard solutions

Reflection on X-factor

(Em)passion

My passion is designing beautiful, user-friendly websites that stay with you after you leave.

Collaboration

I worked in a great group where communication and improving each other's work went smoothly.

Enterprising & Innovative

I added many extras to the site that provide fun, innovative effects beyond the basic requirements.

Multi- & Disciplinarity

I learned to view and solve problems from both a graphic design (visual) and front-end (technical) perspective.

Sustainable

I regularly work with templates so they can be reused for faster and better results in the future.
